SpeakUp Nepal

Challenge

Engineering colleges in Nepal often focus only on traditional academics. Students graduate with theory but have very few chances to work on real-world problems. Research culture is weak, innovation is rare, and industries feel a gap between what students learn and what they actually need in practice. Without a strong link between classroom learning and industry challenges, students miss out on building confidence and practical skills.

Solution

The Research and Innovation Unit (RIU) at Advanced College of Engineering and Management (ACEM) was set up to change this. RIU acts as a bridge between students, faculty, and industries. It creates a space where students can work on projects, test new ideas, and collaborate with experts.

Through workshops, training sessions, and research projects, RIU encourages students to go beyond textbooks and build things that matter. The unit also builds partnerships with local companies, NGOs, and international institutions to bring real-world problems into the classroom.

Impact

RIU has quickly become one of the most active hubs of student innovation in Nepal. Students trained under RIU have built AI projects, worked on 3D printing, designed software tools, and contributed to civic-tech solutions. Many of these projects have gone on to win recognition and even attract industry partnerships.

More importantly, RIU has started a culture shift. Students now see research not as something far away or only for professors, but as something they can do while studying. This confidence has helped them secure internships, jobs, and even start their own ventures.
